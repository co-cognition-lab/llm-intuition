# LLM Global Competition Landscape v4

> 🚧 Coming Soon — Expected Release: Late May 2026

## What This Report Is

The **LLM Global Competition Landscape v4** is Co-Cognition Lab's flagship strategic analysis that bridges cognitive theory with real-world market intelligence. It applies the research framework developed in [Project 1: LLM Intuition](/en/main) — including intuition subtypes, constitutive degradation, and the human-first protocol — to map how the global LLM race is actually unfolding across strategic, technical, and cognitive dimensions.

In other words: this is where rigorous cognitive science meets the competitive reality of the world's most consequential technology race.

## Why It Matters

The LLM landscape is not a single race — it is at least four races running in parallel, each with different finish lines, different rules, and different implications for human cognition. Most competitive analyses focus on benchmarks and market share. This report asks a deeper question: **Which trajectory is actually reachable for which kind of cognition?** The answer shapes not only investment and policy decisions, but also how we should position human intelligence relative to rapidly evolving AI systems.

## Four Analytical Lines

The report is structured around four intersecting analytical dimensions:

**1. Four-Trajectory Competitive Mapping**
- **Closed-source Big 3** — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google: frontier model concentration, proprietary scaling
- **World Model** — embodied reasoning, physical-world grounding, simulation-based approaches
- **Hardware-bound** — inference-optimized architectures, edge deployment, efficiency-first scaling
- **Open Core** — open-weight ecosystems, community-driven development, transparent architectures

Each trajectory carries distinct implications for capability boundaries and strategic vulnerability.

**2. Intuition Subtype Reachability Mapping**
Building on the four intuition subtypes identified in Project 1, this analysis maps which trajectories can plausibly reach which cognitive functions — and where fundamental ceilings persist regardless of scale.

**3. Asymmetric Degradation Risk Ranking**
Not all failure modes are equal. The report ranks how each trajectory risks degrading along specific capability dimensions, identifying where today's strengths may become tomorrow's blind spots.

**4. Eight-Scenario Probability Projection**
A structured forward projection synthesizing the above into eight distinct, probability-weighted scenarios for the 2026–2028 competitive landscape — designed for decision-makers who need actionable foresight, not speculation.
